## The TNRA Way

The original TNRA group has lasted for over 23 years. This is in no small part because of the 
light touch it has in our busy lives, while still providing a meaningful structure for daily, weekly, monthly and 
yearly activities. It's this loose structure that provides the cadence for a TNRA group.

We recommend a group size between 4 and 6 people. In our experience, with more than 6 people, the group gets bogged
down and with less than 4, there aren't enough member for a good rotation of calls between members.

### Cadence
:: Daily
You call a member of your TNRA group each day. You receive a call from a different member of your TNRA group each day.
:: Weekly
You post weekly using this application. Our group has between 8pm Friday and 8pm Saturday to post each week.
:: Monthly
Your TNRA group meets monthly. At this meeting, each member will have an opportunity to review their commitments from
the last month and make new commitments for the upcoming month.
:: Yearly
Once a year, your TNRA group goes on a weekend retreat. You prepare two documents: "The Year in Review" (to discuss 
accomplishments and misses from the previous year) and "A Vision for You" (to discuss commitments and goals for the 
next year).
:::

## What your group gets
:: 📝 Structured weekly posts
Every member fills out the same format: intro, kryptonite, commitments, best & worst across life domains, and configurable stats. Consistency builds accountability.
:: 🔔 Optional Slack integration
When a member finishes a post, your group's Slack channel gets a notification with a secure deep link. No more wondering who posted.
:: 🔒 Encrypted at rest
All post content and stats are encrypted with AES-256-GCM. Your group's entries don't leave the encrypted database unless you choose to share them.

## Anatomy of a Post
:: Intro
The intro section of a post has three sections:
* "What I don't want you to know" - that thing during your week that you'd rather not talk about - but you will to get the support you need.
* "Kryptonite" - that thing that is most in your way
* "What and When" - specific list of actionable commitments you will do in the upcoming week
:: Personal, Family, Professional
For each of Personal, Family, and Professional categories, you list what went **best** and **worst** over the course of
the past week.
:: Stats
The admin of each TNRA group defines a set of stats that you track in the app. This will be a number that you tick up
each time you do it. The original TNRA group uses:
* read
* pray
* meditate
* sponsor
* calls
* exercise
* meetings

Your group can decide on the stats every member will track. Additionally, each member can define their own personal
stats.
